Artificial Turf Around Pools: Benefits and Installation Guide

By Dusty Broadhead | February 26, 2026

Pool surrounds are one of the trickiest landscaping challenges in Georgia. Natural grass around a pool turns into a muddy, patchy mess—constant splash-out, chlorinated water, foot traffic, and shade from pool equipment create conditions where grass simply can’t thrive. Artificial turf solves these problems beautifully, but pool-adjacent installations require specific considerations that differ from a standard lawn.

Why Turf Works Around Pools

The benefits of turf around a swimming pool are practical and aesthetic. No grass clippings blowing into the pool—this alone is worth the investment if you’ve spent countless hours skimming your pool after mowing. No mud tracked onto the pool deck or into the house after rainstorms. A consistently green, soft surface for lounging, playing, and walking barefoot. Excellent drainage that handles splash-out and rain equally well. And reduced insect habitat compared to natural grass, which matters during Georgia’s mosquito season.

The visual impact is stunning. A pool surrounded by perfectly green turf looks resort-like, and it stays that way 365 days a year without any of the maintenance that natural grass demands.

Chlorine and Pool Chemical Considerations

The most common concern about pool-side turf is whether chlorinated water damages it. The honest answer: standard pool chlorine levels (1-3 ppm) don’t damage quality turf fibers. Polyethylene and polypropylene fibers are chemically resistant to chlorine at concentrations far higher than any residential pool maintains.

However, there are a few caveats. Concentrated pool chemicals—if you spill undiluted chlorine or acid directly on turf, it can cause localized damage. Keep chemical storage away from turf areas and rinse any spills immediately. Salt water pools are also fine—the salt concentration in a saltwater pool is well within what turf materials can handle.

The bigger concern is actually splash-out volume. Pools generate a lot of water—kids jumping in, diving, water features—and that water needs to drain through the turf and away from the pool structure. Proper drainage design is critical.

Drainage Design for Pool Surrounds

Pool-adjacent turf needs robust drainage for several reasons: constant splash-out keeps the area wetter than a normal lawn, pool water needs to drain away from the pool structure to prevent undermining, and the area must dry quickly to prevent standing water where people walk barefoot.

Our approach includes sloping the turf base away from the pool coping at a minimum 2% grade, installing a perimeter drain between the pool coping and the turf area, using high-drainage-rate base material (crushed stone with no fines), and selecting turf products with maximum drainage capacity (60+ inches per hour).

Safety Considerations

Turf around pools needs to be safe for wet, barefoot traffic. Quality artificial turf actually provides better traction when wet than many pool deck materials like smooth concrete or polished stone. The fiber texture creates grip, and the infill provides stability. That said, we recommend shorter pile heights (1.25-1.75 inches) around pools because they drain faster and dry quicker, reducing slip risk.

Heat is also a safety consideration. Pool areas are often in full sun, and turf will get warm. We recommend incorporating shade structures, trees, or sail shades over the turf lounge areas. A quick hose rinse cools the surface immediately if it gets too warm.

Installation Details

The critical detail in pool-surround installations is the transition between the pool coping and the turf. This joint needs to be watertight (preventing water from getting behind the coping), secure (the turf edge can’t pull away and create a trip hazard), and clean-looking (a visible gap or bunched turf next to a pool looks terrible).

We use a combination of adhesive and concealed edge pins to secure turf tightly against pool coping. The turf is cut precisely to the coping profile—including curves around kidney-shaped pools, freeform designs, and straight edges on rectangular pools. This is one area where professional installation makes a huge difference over DIY.

Design Ideas for Pool Areas

  • Full turf surround: Replace the entire grass area around the pool with turf for a seamless green look. Combine with a paver or stone pool deck for the immediate pool edge.
  • Turf lounge zones: Create defined turf areas for lounging with sun beds, separated from paver dining and cooking zones.
  • Tropical resort feel: Turf combined with palm trees, tropical plantings, and boulder features creates a backyard that feels like a vacation destination.
  • Sport court adjacent: Turf around the pool connecting to a turf sport court or putting green creates a unified outdoor recreation area.

Maintenance Around Pools

Pool-adjacent turf requires slightly more maintenance than a standard turf lawn. Rinse the turf weekly to wash away any chemical residue from splash-out. Brush against the grain monthly to prevent matting from heavy foot traffic. Check infill levels quarterly—splash-out can wash infill toward the edges over time. And keep pool chemicals stored well away from the turf area.

The maintenance is still dramatically less than natural grass around a pool—no mowing near pool equipment, no fertilizing (which can alter pool chemistry), and no fighting the losing battle of keeping grass alive in a constantly wet, chlorinated zone.
