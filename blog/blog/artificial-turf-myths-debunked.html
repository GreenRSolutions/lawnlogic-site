10 Artificial Turf Myths Debunked by a 20-Year Installer

By Dusty Broadhead | March 16, 2026

After installing artificial turf for more than 20 years across North Georgia, I've heard every myth, concern, and misconception about synthetic grass. Many people make decisions based on false information. I want to set the record straight with real facts from someone who's been doing this since before artificial turf was mainstream.

Myth #1: "Artificial Turf Gets Too Hot"

The Reality: Quality artificial turf does not get excessively hot. In Georgia's summer sun, premium turf may be slightly warmer than natural grass, but modern turf technology keeps temperatures manageable. I've measured temperatures on installed turf—they're typically within comfortable ranges for play and barefoot use. Budget turfs might get hotter, but premium products are engineered for our climate.

Myth #2: "Artificial Turf Looks Plastic"

The Reality: This was true 15+ years ago. Modern artificial turf is incredibly realistic. Quality turf has color variation, natural texture, and realistic blade structure. When properly installed, it's indistinguishable from natural grass at normal viewing distances. Budget turf looks cheap, but premium turf looks authentic.

Myth #3: "Artificial Turf Doesn't Drain"

The Reality: Properly installed artificial turf drains better than most natural lawns. Professional installation includes substrate design that moves water away efficiently. Improper installation causes drainage problems, but that's installer error, not a turf issue. Our installations drain completely within minutes of rain.

Myth #4: "You Can't Have Sports Fields with Artificial Turf"

The Reality: Professional sports use artificial turf. Major soccer fields, football stadiums, and training facilities globally use synthetic turf. Georgia schools and universities increasingly choose artificial turf for athletic fields specifically because it handles heavy use and provides consistent playing conditions.

Myth #5: "Artificial Turf Is Bad for the Environment"

The Reality: Artificial turf is actually environmentally superior to many natural lawn practices. It requires zero pesticides, zero fertilizers, zero water irrigation, and eliminates fuel consumption from mowing. Over a 15-year lifespan, the environmental benefit is substantial. It's one of the most eco-friendly lawn options available.

Myth #6: "Artificial Turf Needs Constant Replacement"

The Reality: Quality artificial turf lasts 15-20 years with minimal maintenance. Natural grass needs constant recovery, reseeding, and total renovation every 5-10 years. Turf's lifespan is actually longer and more predictable than natural grass.

Myth #7: "Artificial Turf Smells Bad"

The Reality: Properly installed turf doesn't smell. Pet-friendly turf specifically handles pet waste with specialized drainage. Regular maintenance (occasional rinsing) keeps turf fresh. Smell issues only happen with improper installation or inadequate maintenance. Quality installations smell like nothing—just fresh grass.

Myth #8: "Dogs Hate Artificial Turf"

The Reality: Dogs love artificial turf. They play on it constantly without hesitation. Pet-friendly turf is specifically designed for dog use. I've watched countless dogs go crazy with joy on newly installed turf. This myth has no basis in reality.

Myth #9: "Artificial Turf Installation Is Cheap So Don't Bother"

The Reality: If you're paying cheap prices, you're getting cheap results. Professional installation costs $10-15 per square foot because proper substrate preparation, professional grading, and quality materials cost money. Budget installations fail. You get what you pay for.

Myth #10: "Natural Grass Is Cheaper Than Artificial Turf"

The Reality: This is the biggest myth. Over 15 years, artificial turf typically costs less than natural grass: - Natural grass: $15,000+ in maintenance, repairs, and renovations - Artificial turf: $8,000-12,000 installation, minimal maintenance - Natural grass loses every time financially

The Bottom Line

After 20+ years, I've seen artificial turf evolve from questionable technology to superior landscaping solution. Modern turf is better for your property, better for the environment, better for your wallet, and better for your quality of life than natural grass. The myths keep people from making smart decisions.

Don't make decisions based on outdated information or myths. Talk to someone who's actually installing turf and living with the results. Our experience speaks for itself.
