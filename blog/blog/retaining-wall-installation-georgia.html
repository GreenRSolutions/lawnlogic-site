Retaining Wall Installation in Georgia: Materials and Costs

By Dusty Broadhead | March 16, 2026

North Georgia's terrain is deceptive. While it looks manageable, many properties have significant grade changes that create drainage challenges and limit usable outdoor space. I've spent two decades solving these problems with properly designed and installed retaining walls. A well-built retaining wall isn't just functional—it transforms a property's appearance, increases usable space, and can dramatically improve drainage. But do it wrong, and you're looking at failure within a few years. Let me share what I've learned.

Why Georgia Properties Need Retaining Walls

Georgia's red clay soil and substantial rainfall create specific challenges. Properties with slopes shed water rapidly, creating erosion. Flat-to-sloped properties accumulate water at the bottom, creating soggy unusable zones. Retaining walls solve both problems while creating level terraces for entertaining, planting, or building.

At LawnLogic Turf, we've completed hundreds of retaining wall projects across Georgia. The best ones combine proper engineering with beautiful finishing that complements the property's design.

Material Options for Georgia Retaining Walls

Natural Stone

Stacked fieldstone or cut granite blocks offer timeless beauty. Cost runs $40-$80 per linear foot installed, depending on height and stone availability. Stone walls can last 30+ years when properly constructed. The challenge: they require experienced installation to look good and function properly. Water management is critical—improper drainage causes failure.

Concrete Blocks/Retaining Wall Units

Modern concrete retaining wall systems are engineered units designed to interlock and manage water pressure. Popular brands include Allan Block, Versa-Lok, and others. Cost runs $25-$50 per linear foot installed. They're straightforward to install, come in multiple finishes and colors, and perform reliably in Georgia's climate.

Timber Retaining Walls

Treated lumber or composite materials are economical ($15-$30 per linear foot), but durability in Georgia's moisture-heavy climate is limited. Timber walls typically last 10-15 years before requiring replacement. For temporary solutions or small applications, timber can work, but it's not ideal for permanent installations.

Segmental Retaining Walls (SRW)

These are engineered stacked blocks that create walls 2-15 feet high. SRW systems are strong, aesthetically modern, and engineered to handle soil pressures. They cost $30-$60 per linear foot and offer excellent durability. Most commercial installations in Georgia use SRW systems.

Critical Installation Factors for Georgia Properties

Drainage is Everything

This is where most DIY or poor-quality installations fail. Soil behind a retaining wall contains water. That water creates pressure against the wall. Without proper drainage, the wall fails. We install perforated drainage pipes behind every wall, gravel-filled drainage zones, and French drains to manage water properly. In Georgia's clay soils, this drainage work is non-negotiable.

Foundation and Base Preparation

A retaining wall is only as good as what it sits on. We excavate to undisturbed soil, create a level compacted base, and often use crushed stone foundations. Proper base prep adds cost but prevents failure. Corners and angles are critical—they take enormous pressure and need reinforcement.

Height Considerations

Walls under 4 feet are relatively straightforward. Walls 4-8 feet require engineered design and may need building permits. Walls over 8 feet usually require professional engineering. We've completed walls up to 14 feet in North Georgia, but they're designed with proper geotextiles, reinforcement, and drainage systems.

Permitting and Codes

Kennesaw and North Georgia municipalities have different requirements. Walls over 4 feet typically need permits. Walls within 10 feet of property lines may require engineering. We handle permitting and coordinate with local authorities as part of our installation process.

Cost Expectations for Georgia Properties

A typical retaining wall installation costs $30-$70 per linear foot depending on height, material, and site conditions. A 50-foot wall, 4 feet tall, with concrete units and proper drainage might cost $1,500-$3,500. That same wall in natural stone could be $2,000-$4,000. Complex sites with poor drainage or challenging soil conditions cost more.

These aren't insignificant expenses, but the ROI is substantial. A well-designed retaining wall increases usable property, improves drainage, adds visual appeal, and increases property value.

Design Considerations

The best retaining walls integrate with overall landscape design. We often design walls to create level entertaining platforms, define garden areas, or manage water drainage to create functional planting zones above and below the wall. A wall becomes an asset, not just a functional necessity.

Material selection affects appearance significantly. Natural stone offers rustic elegance. Modern concrete units provide clean contemporary lines. We choose materials that complement the home's architecture and surrounding landscape.

Maintenance and Longevity

Properly installed walls require minimal maintenance. Stone may need occasional mortar repair. Concrete units rarely need attention. Drainage systems should be inspected periodically to ensure they're functioning. Quality installations should last 20-30+ years.

Common Georgia Mistakes

I see these failures repeatedly: walls built without drainage systems collapse in heavy rain. Walls on unstable soil shift and fail. Walls without proper foundation settle unevenly. Walls built without permits create legal liability. The money saved by cutting corners always becomes expensive when failure occurs.

When to Call the Professionals

Walls under 3 feet on stable, well-draining property might be DIY projects. Anything taller or more complex should be professionally designed and installed.
