Artificial Turf Around Pools: Benefits and Best Practices

By Dusty Broadhead | March 16, 2026

Pool areas present unique challenges for landscaping. They need surfaces that are slip-resistant, drain quickly, withstand constant chlorine exposure, and require minimal maintenance. After 20+ years installing turf around Georgia pools, I can tell you definitively: artificial turf is often the best solution for poolside areas. The combination of safety, functionality, and low maintenance creates a superior pool environment.

Why Artificial Turf Works Around Pools

Pool decking traditionally means pavers or concrete, but artificial turf offers distinct advantages. Modern pool-specific turf is engineered to handle chlorine exposure, intense sun, wet conditions, and barefoot comfort.

Safety Benefits

  • Slip resistance: Proper pool turf has textured backing that provides grip even when wet
  • Soft surface: Kids running poolside won't slip as easily as on wet pavers
  • Impact cushioning: Falls are softer than onto hard pavers or concrete

Drainage Advantages

Properly installed artificial turf drains 20-30 inches per hour. Water moves through the turf, down through infill, and away via base layers. No standing water means no algae growth, no mold, and no safety hazards. This is superior to many paver installations around Georgia pools.

Chlorine and Chemical Resistance

Quality pool turf is manufactured to resist chlorine, salt water, and common pool chemicals. The fibers don't degrade from chemical exposure, and the backing is selected for chemical resistance. This matters—cheap turf will deteriorate quickly around pools.

Installation Around Pools

Pool installations require specific attention: proper slope for water movement away from the pool, secure edge treatment to prevent tripping hazards, and adequate drainage. We use specialized pool-grade turf from manufacturers who explicitly engineer for pool environments.

Common Pool Turf Specifications

  • Polyethylene fibers (chlorine-resistant)
  • Perforated backing (water drainage)
  • Sand and rubber infill (maintains grip when wet)
  • Slope away from pool (standard 1-2%)

Maintenance Around Pools

Pool turf maintenance is similar to standard turf: brushing monthly, rinsing regularly to remove chlorine residue and algae spores, and occasional professional cleaning. The regular rinsing actually becomes easier because you're already managing pool area cleanup.

Best Practices for Georgia Pool Areas

  • Choose pool-specific turf not standard lawn turf
  • Ensure excellent drainage in our humid climate
  • Install proper slope away from water features
  • Use non-slip infill combination for safety
  • Plan for direct hose rinsing to remove chlorine
