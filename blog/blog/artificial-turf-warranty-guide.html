Understanding Artificial Turf Warranties: What's Covered?

By Dusty Broadhead | March 16, 2026

When you invest in artificial turf installation for your Georgia property, warranty coverage matters significantly. But warranty documents are often confusing, full of exclusions and technical language. After 20+ years in this business, I've fielded countless warranty questions from confused homeowners. Let me demystify what warranty actually means and what really matters for your installation.

Warranty Types in Artificial Turf

Manufacturer's Warranty

This covers defects in the turf product itself. Manufacturers guarantee that the fibers won't prematurely fade, deteriorate, or fail under normal use. Typical coverage is 10-15 years, though premium products offer up to 20 years. This warranty covers manufacturing defects only—not improper installation or maintenance issues.

Installation Warranty

Professional installers typically provide warranty on their workmanship. This covers improper seaming, base preparation errors, or installation mistakes. Quality installers guarantee their work for 2-5 years. This is important—it means if problems result from installation mistakes, the installer covers repairs.

Combined Warranty

The best protection combines both: manufacturer's product warranty plus installer's workmanship warranty. This comprehensive coverage protects against both product defects and installation errors.

What Manufacturer Warranties Typically Cover

Fiber Deterioration

Manufacturers guarantee the turf fibers won't prematurely deteriorate in normal conditions. If fibers break down before the warranty period, manufacturer covers replacement.

Blade Loss

Some turf experiences blade loss (fibers pulling away from the backing). Quality products guarantee minimal blade loss. Excessive blade loss within warranty period is covered.

Backing Defects

The backing (the material holding fibers) should remain stable and not degrade. Backing failures within warranty are covered.

Seam Separation

Some products guarantee seams won't separate due to manufacturing defects. This is important—defective seams can fail within years. Proper warranty covers this.

What Warranties Typically DO NOT Cover

Normal Wear and Tear

Heavy use causes normal wear. Warranties don't cover replacement of worn areas from normal use. This is expected over a product's lifespan.

Improper Installation

Installation errors aren't manufacturer's responsibility. This is why installer warranty matters—they should cover their mistakes. If you DIY-install and have problems, manufacturer won't cover installation errors.

Inadequate Maintenance

Warranties assume basic maintenance. If you neglect the turf, causing problems, warranty doesn't cover it. Regular rinsing and basic care are required.

Pet Damage or Chemical Burns

Damage from pet urine concentration or chemical exposure typically isn't covered. Manufacturers can't guarantee against every possible damage source.

Improper Base Preparation

If base preparation causes failures (settling, water pooling, etc.), that's not manufacturer responsibility. This is why professional installation with proper base work matters.

Environmental Extremes

Extreme conditions beyond manufacturer specifications often aren't covered. For example, prolonged flooding or extreme heat exposure might exceed warranty terms.

Important Warranty Conditions

Professional Installation Requirement

Most quality warranties require professional installation. DIY installations void warranty. This is critical—it means if you install yourself, you lose warranty protection entirely.

Proper Maintenance

Warranties require regular maintenance per manufacturer guidelines. Typically this means occasional rinsing and basic care. Neglecting maintenance can void warranty coverage.

Documentation and Proof of Installation

You need documentation showing professional installation to claim warranty benefits. Keep all installation paperwork and warranty documents. They're essential if you need to file a claim.

Time Limits on Claims

Warranties have time limits for reporting issues. If you discover a problem, report it promptly. Delaying too long might disqualify you from coverage.

Transferability for Homeowners

Quality warranties are often transferable to subsequent homeowners. This is valuable for property resale—it adds to property value and appeal to buyers. Document this carefully if you sell.

What to Look for in Warranty

Length

Longer is better. 10-15 year manufacturer warranties are standard. Premium products offer 20+ years.

What's Specifically Covered

Get clarity on what's included. Fiber fade? Blade loss? Seam separation? What are the specific guarantees?

What's Excluded

Read the exclusions carefully. Understanding what's NOT covered prevents disappointment if issues arise.

Installation Requirements

Professional installation requirement protects you—it ensures the product is installed properly to perform as designed.

Maintenance Requirements

Understand the maintenance obligations. Most require basic care only, but clarify specifics.

Real Warranty Example

I completed an installation where the customer discovered seam separation three years later (within warranty). Because we used a quality product with proper installation warranty, we contacted the manufacturer. Seam separation was a manufacturing defect covered by warranty. Manufacturer covered the repair cost. Without warranty, the customer would have paid $1,500+ out of pocket.

Questions to Ask Before Installation

Before committing to artificial turf installation, ask: What's the manufacturer warranty? What's the installer warranty? What documentation will I receive? Is warranty transferable? What maintenance is required to maintain warranty? At LawnLogic Turf, we're transparent about warranty details and provide comprehensive documentation with every installation.

Warranty Peace of Mind

Quality warranty coverage is peace of mind. It means if problems arise from manufacturing defects or installation mistakes, you have recourse. This is one reason professional installation justifies its cost—you get both superior installation quality and warranty protection.
