7 Common Mistakes to Avoid When Installing Artificial Turf

By Dusty Broadhead | March 16, 2026

I've been installing artificial turf in North Georgia for over 20 years. In that time, I've seen countless mistakes—some made by DIY homeowners, others by inexperienced installers. These mistakes cost money, create problems, and result in artificial turf that doesn't perform as expected. I want to share the seven most common mistakes I see, so you can avoid them.

Mistake #1: Poor Substrate Preparation

This is the #1 killer of artificial turf installations. The substrate—the material underneath the turf—is critical. If it's not properly compacted, not properly leveled, or using cheap materials, your turf will fail.

I've seen installations where the substrate wasn't compacted properly. The result? Settling. The turf surface develops low spots and uneven areas within a year. In Georgia's heat, improper substrate leads to soft areas that compress under foot traffic.

The right way: Professional-grade substrate, properly compacted in layers, with precise laser grading. This is what LawnLogic Turf does on every installation.

Mistake #2: Inadequate Drainage Design

Georgia gets heavy rain. If your artificial turf installation doesn't include proper drainage design, you'll have puddles, standing water, and potentially damage to surrounding structures.

Proper drainage requires slope (typically 1-2% grade), permeable substrate, and proper substrate design that allows water to flow away from your home. Many budget installations skip this critical element.

The right way: Professional grading, properly sloped substrate layers, and design that directs water away from your home and any low areas.

Mistake #3: Choosing Wrong Turf for the Application

Not all artificial turf is the same. Using residential-grade turf for a high-traffic area will fail. Using putting green turf for a regular lawn looks weird and wears oddly. Turf selection matters enormously.

I've seen DIY installations where homeowners bought the cheapest turf they could find. The result looks artificial, cheap, and often doesn't perform well. Color fades. Blades compress. It just doesn't last.

The right way: Select premium turf appropriate for your specific application—residential, high-traffic, sports, or specialty uses. Professional color and blade selection makes enormous difference.

Mistake #4: Improper Seaming and Sealing

Seams are the weak point of artificial turf installation. If seams aren't properly sealed, you get:

  • Weed growth along seams
  • Water seeping through seam areas
  • Seams unraveling over time
  • Visible seams that look cheap

Proper seaming requires: cutting pieces precisely, overlapping correctly, applying professional-grade seam adhesive, and proper curing time. This can't be rushed.

The right way: Professional seaming with proper overlap, professional-grade adhesive, and adequate curing time before use.

Mistake #5: Inadequate Edge Containment

Turf edges need proper containment. Without it, edges fray, unravel, and look cheap. Water also wicks underneath unsecured edges, undermining your installation.

Budget installations often skip professional edging, just cutting the turf and hoping for the best. Within a year, edges deteriorate.

The right way: Professional edging systems that contain the turf, prevent water infiltration, and create clean finished edges that look like intentional design.

Mistake #6: Not Planning for Drainage of Pet Waste

If you have pets, drainage design is critical. Poor drainage leads to puddling, odor issues, and pet waste not draining properly. This creates actual problems in your yard.

Pet-friendly artificial turf requires special substrate design and proper slope. Many cheap installations don't account for pet use at all.

The right way: If you have or plan to have pets, specify pet-friendly turf and drainage design during installation. LawnLogic Turf specializes in this.

Mistake #7: Insufficient Ground Preparation Before Installation

What's under the artificial turf matters. If old roots, debris, or contaminants are left underneath, they cause problems. Proper preparation requires:

  • Removing all old sod and vegetation
  • Clearing debris and roots
  • Grading and leveling the land
  • Installing proper substrate from scratch

I've seen installations where old sod was simply cut and left in place under the new turf. Within a season, it decomposes and creates settling issues.

The right way: Complete ground preparation. Remove everything. Start fresh with professional-grade substrate installed properly.

The Solution: Professional Installation

These mistakes are expensive to fix. Many cost thousands of dollars to repair. The best approach? Professional installation from the start by someone who understands Georgia's climate, proper technique, and what actually works.
