Backyard Transformations: Before and After Gallery Guide

By Dusty Broadhead | March 16, 2026

One of my favorite parts of this job is watching people experience their finished backyards for the first time. After 20+ years of transforming North Georgia properties, I can tell you that the emotional impact of a great backyard renovation is real. Properties that were essentially unused outdoor spaces become family gathering spots, entertaining venues, and genuine escapes from the daily grind. Our gallery showcases dozens of these transformations, and I want to walk you through what makes the best ones work.

Why Before and After Gallery Matters

Photos are more than pretty pictures. They show the realistic potential of your property. They demonstrate what specific materials look like in Georgia sunlight and weather. They provide inspiration. Most importantly, they show real projects we've completed for clients just like you, in your area, dealing with challenges similar to yours.

At LawnLogic Turf, our project gallery is extensive and honest. You're seeing completed work, not renderings or marketing photos. This is what we actually deliver.

Common Transformation Patterns

The Unusable Lawn to Entertainment Zone

This is perhaps the most common transformation we execute. A homeowner has an overgrown, poorly maintained natural grass lawn—often damaged by heavy use, clay-based mud, or difficult shade conditions. We install quality artificial turf throughout, add hardscaping (patio, pathways), and integrate features like fire pits or seating areas. The result: a family suddenly has a backyard they actually use daily.

The Sloped Property Challenge

North Georgia has many properties with significant elevation changes. These are challenging for traditional landscaping—water runs off, grass struggles to grow on slopes, the usable flat space is minimal. We design retaining walls to create level terraces, install artificial turf on the new flat areas, and integrate those with hardscaping. A previously "problem" property becomes a multi-zone entertaining space.

The Shaded Nightmare to Green Space

Properties with mature tree canopy, pergolas, or buildings casting shade present unique challenges for natural grass. Grass dies. We install artificial turf, which thrives in shade (one of its best applications), and often add shade structures or furniture that makes the shaded area desirable rather than a problem to solve.

The Pet-Damaged Yard Rehabilitation

Heavy pet use destroys natural grass. Our solution: dedicated artificial turf areas designed for pet safety and comfort. The rest of the property can remain natural grass or be transformed to hardscaping. The family gets their yard back, and the pets get a space actually designed for their use.

What the Best Transformations Have in Common

Purposeful Design

The best before-and-after projects start with understanding how the family actually wants to use their yard. Casual entertaining? Serious entertaining? Active play space for kids? Quiet retreat for one person? The design flows from that purpose.

Thoughtful Material Selection

Using the right materials for the right application matters enormously. Premium artificial turf for high-traffic areas. Hardscaping that complements the home's architecture. Plantings that work with Georgia's climate. No shortcuts.

Integration of Functional and Beautiful

The best yards work beautifully AND function excellently. They look stunning in photos AND they're genuinely usable by the family. They solve problems (drainage, shade, pet damage) while creating spaces that feel intentional and designed.

Appropriate Scale to the Property

A transformation that works respects the property's size and context. A 2,000-square-foot entertaining zone on a quarter-acre property is overwhelming. The same zone on a one-acre property feels perfect. Good design scales appropriately.

Common Elements in Our Gallery Projects

If you browse our complete project gallery, you'll notice several patterns:

Artificial Turf Coverage: Most successful projects use artificial turf in some quantity. It's the foundation of a low-maintenance, visually consistent yard.

Hardscaped Entertaining Areas: Patios, pathways, and defined zones create structure and usable space.

Shade Integration: Whether from structures or plantings, shade makes outdoor spaces comfortable and usable longer.

Water Features or Fire Pits: These focal points add visual interest and create gathering zones.

Thoughtful Lighting: Evening functionality and ambiance matter. Most of our completed projects include integrated LED lighting.

Budget Ranges in the Gallery

Our projects range from modest $5,000 renovations (small turf areas, basic hardscaping) to comprehensive $50,000+ transformations (multiple zones, premium materials, extensive hardscaping). Each one in the gallery tells you the project scope and investment, helping you understand what's possible at different budget levels.

Regional Considerations in Our Work

Every project in our Kennesaw and North Georgia gallery accounts for regional specifics: Georgia clay soils and drainage challenges, our subtropical climate with hot summers and mild winters, common local pests and plant diseases, HOA requirements in various neighborhoods, available material options and costs specific to our region.

This is why national gallery examples matter less than seeing actual Georgia installations. A beautiful California drought-resistant landscape doesn't work here. Our gallery shows solutions designed for actual North Georgia conditions.
