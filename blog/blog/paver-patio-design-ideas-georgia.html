Paver Patio Design Ideas for Georgia Homes

By Dusty Broadhead | March 16, 2026

Your backyard patio is where outdoor living happens. Family gatherings, evening relaxation, entertaining friends—the right patio design sets the stage for all of it. After 20+ years working on outdoor design in North Georgia, I've learned that a great patio starts with smart paver design combined with properly landscaped surroundings, including quality artificial turf.

Let me share the patio design ideas that work beautifully in our climate and create outdoor spaces people actually use.

Understand Your Patio's Purpose

The first decision is: what will this patio be used for? Your answer shapes everything about the design.

Entertainment-Focused Patios

If you're hosting gatherings, you need adequate space—typically 12-by-16 feet minimum for comfortable movement and seating. Leave space for a grill area separate from the seating zone. Consider shade from an adjacent tree or pergola for comfort during Georgia's hot summers.

Intimate Seating Areas

Small retreat spaces can be intimate and cozy. An 8-by-10 patio with two chairs, a small table, and plantings creates a perfect morning coffee spot. These work beautifully adjacent to larger entertaining patios.

Functional Work Spaces

If you grill frequently or have extensive outdoor cooking plans, design a proper kitchen area with counter space, storage, and movement room. A 10-by-12 area works well for dedicated grilling stations.

Paver Selection and Patterns

Popular Paver Options for Georgia

Concrete Pavers: Affordable and durable. Available in many colors and patterns. They handle Georgia's heat and weather well. They're the most popular choice for residential patios.

Natural Stone (Bluestone, Flagstone): Premium appearance. Beautiful color variation. More expensive but adds luxury to your outdoor space. They develop nice patina over time.

Porcelain Pavers: Extremely durable and low-maintenance. Available in large formats that create clean, modern aesthetics. More expensive upfront but last longer.

Pattern Designs

Running Bond: Simple, classic pattern that's easy on the eye and works with almost any surroundings. Perfect for traditional Georgia homes.

Herringbone: Visually interesting pattern that adds sophistication. Slightly more complex to install but worth it for the visual impact.

Basket Weave: Elegant and formal-looking. Works beautifully in more traditional landscaping designs.

Random/Ashlar: Natural, organic appearance. Great for creating casual, relaxed outdoor spaces. Works well with transitional home styles.

Color Selection for Georgia Homes

Paver color affects how your patio feels and how it performs in our climate.

Light Colors (Tan, Cream, Light Gray)

Reflect Georgia's intense summer sun, keeping the patio cooler. They stay cleaner-looking longer. Light colors work beautifully in front yards and small intimate spaces. They pair perfectly with artificial turf surroundings.

Medium Colors (Gray, Warm Tan)

The sweet spot for most patios. They show dirt less obviously than light pavers but stay cooler than dark colors. Neutral enough to work with any home style. The most popular choice we install.

Dark Colors (Charcoal, Dark Brown)

Create dramatic, sophisticated looks. They can get quite hot in Georgia's summer sun, which is a practical consideration. Use them as accent patterns or borders rather than the entire patio.

Size and Layout Considerations

Patio Dimensions

Too small: Under 8 feet by 10 feet feels cramped even with minimal furniture.

Comfortable: 10 feet by 12 feet accommodates a dining table with comfortable movement.

Spacious: 12 feet by 16 feet or larger allows multiple use zones and abundant entertaining space.

Shape Design

Rectangular: Classic and efficient. Works well against the house. Maximizes usable space.

Square: Creates balance and symmetry. Works well in modern landscapes.

Curved Edges: Soften hard lines. Create organic flow between patio and lawn. More complex to install but worth it for aesthetics.

Integrating Artificial Turf with Patio Design

The space around your patio matters as much as the patio itself. This is where LawnLogic Turf creates magic. Quality artificial turf surroundings make your patio look like a professional landscape design.

Turf Transition Zones

Install artificial turf in a 3-5 foot border around your patio. This creates definition, frames the patio beautifully, and provides a clean, finished look. It's the perfect frame for your hardscape investment.

Planting Beds and Turf

Combine paver patios with landscaping beds filled with plants and mulch. Surround it with artificial turf. This creates visual interest and professional landscaping appearance without needing constant maintenance.

Functional Integration

Artificial turf around patios handles foot traffic that would destroy natural grass. Kids running from patio to play areas don't create worn paths. The turf stays beautiful year-round as the backdrop to your patio investment.

Drainage Considerations

Georgia rain comes heavy and unpredictable. Proper slope and drainage design keeps water from pooling on your patio.

Pavers should slope away from the house at roughly 1/8 inch per foot. Your landscape professional handles this during installation. Proper grading prevents water from pooling on the patio surface or creating issues with your home foundation.

Shade and Comfort

Georgia summers are hot. Most successful patios include shade elements:

  • Pergolas over seating areas (provide partial shade without blocking air circulation)
  • Large patio umbrellas (mobile shade that can be adjusted throughout the day)
  • Existing shade trees positioned to shade afternoon seating (best option if you have established trees)
  • Shade sails (modern, clean look that provides substantial coverage)

Budget Considerations

Budget impacts every design decision. Here's what to expect:

Basic concrete patio: $10-15 per square foot. A 10x12 area is roughly $1,200-1,800.

Quality pavers: $15-25 per square foot. Adds to the installed cost.

Premium natural stone: $25-40+ per square foot.

When you factor in surrounding artificial turf, proper grading, and landscaping, budget $50-100+ per square foot for a complete patio experience. Your investment creates an outdoor room your family will use for years.
