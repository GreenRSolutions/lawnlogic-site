Building the Perfect Dog Run with Artificial Turf

By Dusty Broadhead | March 16, 2026

Dog owners in Georgia face a real problem: natural grass deteriorates rapidly under heavy pet use. Dogs repeatedly urinate in the same spots, creating dead patches. Their digging creates mud holes that wash away in rain. They track mud through the house. The cycle of reseeding, patching, and fighting with your lawn never ends. After two decades of installation work, I've solved this problem hundreds of times with artificial turf dog runs. These spaces keep your pets safe, healthy, and active while maintaining a beautiful yard. Here's what every Georgia dog owner should know.

Why Artificial Turf is Perfect for Dogs

Quality artificial turf tolerates heavy dog use that natural grass cannot. It won't die from repeated urine spots. It won't be destroyed by digging. It dries quickly after rain, eliminating mud. Most importantly, it provides a comfortable, clean surface that's genuinely better for your pets than natural grass.

At LawnLogic Turf, we've installed specialized dog runs for families across North Georgia. Every one of them reports the same thing: their properties look better, their dogs seem happier, and they've eliminated the constant maintenance frustration of managing pet-damaged natural grass.

Design Considerations for Dog Runs

Size Matters

A dedicated dog run should be at least 400-600 square feet for a medium-sized dog, larger for multiple pets or large breeds. But more is always better. We've designed dedicated runs up to 1,200+ square feet—essentially a complete section of the yard where dogs can run, play, and relieve themselves without access to planting areas or the rest of the property.

Shade and Shelter

Georgia summers are hot. A dog run without shade becomes unusable in July and August. We design runs with shade structures—pergolas, shade sails, or trees providing dappled shade. This allows dogs to be outside comfortably most of the year. A water station is essential, particularly during warm months.

Secure Fencing

The turf surface is only part of the equation. Proper fencing prevents escapes and contains your pets. We typically specify sturdy 4-6 foot fencing, with special attention to gaps at the bottom where smaller dogs might squeeze through. If you have diggers, buried fencing that extends underground prevents escape attempts.

Drainage Integration

Dog urine and waste need to drain away from the surface. Quality installations include proper underlayment and drainage systems that manage moisture and odor. We often specify hybrid infill systems or organic infills that are specifically chosen for pet applications because they resist odor buildup better than sand-only systems.

Pet-Specific Turf Selection

Not all artificial turf is suitable for heavy pet use. We specify turf products specifically rated for pet applications. These typically feature:

Textured Blades

Smooth turf can be slippery for dogs, particularly older ones or those with joint issues. Pet-appropriate turf has textured blades that provide better traction for running and playing.

Antimicrobial Properties

Premium pet turfs include antimicrobial treatments in the backing that inhibit bacterial growth and reduce odor. This is a critical feature for dog runs, preventing the smell problems that can occur with basic turf.

Drainage Capacity

Pet turfs are engineered for rapid drainage. We specify perforated backing with excellent water flow to ensure urine drains quickly rather than pooling and creating odor.

Infill Selection for Dog Runs

This is where pet-specific design diverges from standard residential turf. We typically don't use rubber infill in dog runs. Instead, we specify:

Sand-Based Systems

Quality silica sand provides drainage and stability. It doesn't retain moisture or odor like rubber can.

Hybrid Organic Infill

Cork or coconut-based infills combined with sand provide cushioning for joint health while biodegrading safely. These are increasingly popular for pet applications because they're comfortable and don't harbor odors.

Avoid Pure Rubber

Traditional rubber tire infill can absorb and retain odors, particularly problematic for dog runs. We avoid it in pet applications.

Maintenance for Pet Turf

This is where artificial turf truly shines for dog owners. Maintenance is minimal:

Daily: Simple pickup of solid waste (same as you'd do in any yard)

Weekly: Rinse with garden hose to manage urine and keep surface clean

Monthly: Inspect for any damage or infill displacement; light grooming if needed

That's it. No mowing, no reseeding, no fertilizing, no fungicide applications—all the pesticide and chemical treatments required for natural grass maintenance that could harm your pets.

Cost and ROI

A dedicated dog run of 500 square feet with quality pet-specific artificial turf, proper drainage, and shade structure typically costs $4,000-$8,000 installed. This seems significant, but consider what you're avoiding: annual grass seed, fertilizer, pest control chemicals, constant repair and reseeding, the stress of a perpetually damaged lawn. Most dog owners recover the investment within 3-4 years in reduced maintenance expenses and headache.

Combining Dog Run with Whole-Yard Design

The best solutions integrate the dog run into overall landscape design. Perhaps artificial turf covers the entire lawn, with fencing creating a dedicated dog zone. Or a hybrid approach: a smaller dedicated dog run with enhanced containment, while the rest of the property maintains a mix of hardscaping, plantings, and turf that works for family use. We design these solutions to optimize for your specific family needs.

Real Example

I recently worked with a Kennesaw family who had a 2-year-old Golden Retriever destroying their natural grass yard. We designed a 700-square-foot dog run with premium pet turf, pergola shade, and integrated water station. The family also installed artificial turf throughout their entertaining area, creating a completely functional outdoor space. The dog gets a safe, clean play area. The parents get a beautiful yard that's actually usable. Everyone's happier.

Creating the Perfect Space for Your Pets

Your dogs spend significant time in your yard—they deserve a space that's actually designed for them. Quality artificial turf dog runs provide that. They're clean, safe, maintenance-free, and genuinely better for your pets than damaged natural grass could ever be.
