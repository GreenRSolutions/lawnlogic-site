Complete Guide to Artificial Turf Drainage Systems

By Dusty Broadhead | March 29, 2026

Proper drainage is the foundation of every successful artificial turf installation. Georgia's heavy rainfall events make drainage engineering critical. Here's everything you need to know about keeping your turf performing perfectly in any weather.

Why Drainage Matters

Georgia receives an average of 50 inches of rainfall annually, with intense summer thunderstorms that can dump inches in hours. Without proper drainage, water pools on the surface, creating puddles, odors, and potential base erosion. Our engineered drainage systems handle even the heaviest Georgia storms.

Our Drainage System Components

  • Graded sub-base directing water to drainage points
  • 4-6 inches of compacted crushed stone aggregate base
  • Perforated drain pipes in low areas and along property lines
  • Drainage-optimized turf backing allowing 30+ inches per hour flow
  • French drain connections to existing property drainage
  • Catch basins for properties with significant water management needs

Common Drainage Problems We Solve

Many properties have existing drainage issues that natural grass masks. During installation, we identify and correct grading problems, standing water areas, and improper runoff patterns. Clients frequently tell us their yards actually drain better after turf installation than they ever did with natural grass.
