Landscape Lighting and Artificial Turf: Design Tips

By Dusty Broadhead | March 16, 2026

The best outdoor spaces aren't just beautiful during the day—they shine in the evening. After 20+ years designing landscapes with artificial turf, I've learned that proper lighting transforms outdoor spaces. Well-designed landscape lighting combined with quality artificial turf creates stunning evening environments that extend your outdoor living time and dramatically improve your property's nighttime appearance.

Let me share how to combine landscape lighting and artificial turf for maximum impact.

Why Lighting Matters with Artificial Turf

Creates Evening Ambiance

Lighting transforms your yard into an inviting evening retreat. Artificial turf provides the perfect complement—the turf looks beautiful in daylight, and proper lighting makes it luminous and inviting in the evening.

Extends Outdoor Enjoyment

Good lighting means your yard is usable after sunset. Evening gatherings, quiet relaxation, and family time can happen in beautifully lit outdoor spaces.

Enhances Home Value

Professional landscape lighting significantly increases perceived property value. Combined with manicured artificial turf, your home looks like a luxury property.

Path and Accent Lighting

Illuminated Pathways

Low-voltage path lights along walkways guide movement through your yard and create visual interest. Space lights 4-6 feet apart along garden paths. This combines safety with beauty.

Step and Edge Lighting

Lights on steps and patio edges enhance safety while creating elegant visual definition. This is especially important if your artificial turf borders higher surfaces.

Uplighting Trees and Features

Uplighting from ground level toward trees, shrubs, or architectural features creates drama. Trees lit from below look spectacular. This technique adds depth and dimension to your landscape.

Patio and Gathering Space Lighting

Ambient Overhead Lighting

Installed in pergolas or overhead structures, this lighting creates comfortable gathering spaces. Dimmable LED systems let you adjust intensity for different moods.

String and Decorative Lighting

String lights overhead create festive, intimate atmospheres. Café-style strings or bistro lighting looks elegant and creates cozy gathering areas on artificial turf patios.

Accent and Feature Lighting

Direct lights on specific features—fountains, sculptures, or architectural elements—draw attention and create focal points. This creates visual interest in evening landscapes.

Color Temperature and Mood

Warm vs. Cool Light

Warm Light (2700K-3000K): Creates relaxing, intimate atmosphere. Perfect for gathering spaces and patio areas. Warm light makes artificial turf appear more natural and inviting.

Cool Light (4000K+): Creates modern, crisp appearance. Works for contemporary designs. Makes colors appear more vibrant in the evening.

Choosing Your Temperature

Most residential installations use warm lighting for comfort and relaxation. This pairs beautifully with artificial turf, creating natural-looking evening spaces.

Lighting Design Principles

Layered Lighting Approach

Combine different lighting types:

  • Ambient lighting (overall illumination)
  • Task lighting (functional areas like grills or seating)
  • Accent lighting (focal points and features)
  • Decorative lighting (mood and aesthetics)

Avoiding Over-Lighting

More light isn't always better. Over-lit yards look garish and eliminate the ambiance evening lighting creates. Use strategic placement and dimmable systems for control.

Light Positioning

Position lights to avoid creating harsh shadows or direct glare. Downward and angled lighting looks more professional than bright overhead fixtures.

Artificial Turf Highlighting Techniques

Turf Color Under Different Lighting

Artificial turf looks different under evening lighting. Quality warm lighting makes turf appear natural and inviting. Test lighting before committing to final design.

Edge Lighting Contrast

Lighting the edges between artificial turf and hardscape creates clean visual separation. This makes turf installation look more intentional and professional.

Surrounding Planting Illumination

Light ornamental plantings around turf areas. This frames the turf beautifully and creates depth in your evening landscape.

Practical Lighting System Choices

Low-Voltage Systems

Safer and easier to install than high-voltage. Perfect for residential applications. 12V systems are standard for landscape lighting.

LED Technology

Energy-efficient, long-lasting, and creating beautiful light quality. LED costs more upfront but saves money over years of operation.

Programmable and Smart Systems

Modern systems let you program lighting scenes and control them remotely. Adjust intensity and color temperature for different occasions.
