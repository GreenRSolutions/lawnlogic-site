How Much Does Artificial Turf Cost? Complete Pricing Breakdown

By Dusty Broadhead | March 15, 2026

One of the most common questions I get from homeowners is: "What's this going to cost?" And I get it—artificial turf is a significant investment. But here's the problem: most people only look at the upfront installation cost and miss the big picture.

I've been installing artificial turf for over 20 years, and I've seen families make decisions based on incomplete information. Some regret not doing it sooner. Others wish they'd understood the long-term savings. In this guide, I'm breaking down everything from material costs to installation, and most importantly, showing you the real return on investment over time.

The Basic Cost Breakdown: What You're Actually Paying For

When you get a quote for artificial turf installation, there are several components involved. Let me walk through what each part costs and why.

Material Costs: The Turf Itself

Artificial turf ranges wildly in quality and price. You'll see quotes from $3 to $15+ per square foot for just the material. Here's what affects that range:

  • Entry-level turf ($3-6/sq ft): These are lower-quality synthetic blends. They look plastic-y, don't feel natural, and fade faster. In Georgia's intense sun, these often deteriorate noticeably within 5-7 years. Not worth it in my opinion.
  • Mid-range turf ($6-10/sq ft): This is what most residential installations use. Good balance of durability, appearance, and cost. These look natural, feel realistic, and hold up well in Georgia's climate for 12-15 years.
  • Premium turf ($10-15+/sq ft): High-end luxury brands with superior UV stability, softer blades, and backing technologies that mimic real grass. These last 15-20 years and look exceptional. Worth it if budget allows.

At LawnLogic Turf, we typically recommend mid-range to premium options. You're going to live with this for a decade or more—the slightly higher material cost pays dividends in longevity and performance.

Underlayment and Drainage

This is where a lot of budget-conscious installers cut corners, and it's a mistake. Quality underlayment costs $1-3 per square foot, but it's critical. Here's why:

  • Provides proper drainage (crucial in Georgia's wet winters and summer storms)
  • Prevents weed growth underneath
  • Prolongs turf lifespan by preventing moisture damage
  • Reduces odor in pet turf installations
  • Creates a comfortable, stable surface

We always use quality underlayment. It's non-negotiable for Georgia properties.

Installation Labor

This is where costs vary most based on your property's condition. A simple, flat installation with good drainage already in place might be $2-4 per square foot in labor. A complex job with extensive grading, drainage issues, or tight spaces could run $4-8+ per square foot.

Factors that increase labor costs:

  • Poor existing drainage requiring corrective work
  • Heavy clay compaction needing removal and replacement
  • Tight spaces around decks, landscaping, and features
  • Sloped terrain
  • Removal of existing grass and old landscaping

Real-World Pricing Examples

Example 1: 2,000 sq ft Basic Residential Installation

  • Mid-range turf: 2,000 × $7.50 = $15,000
  • Underlayment: 2,000 × $2 = $4,000
  • Installation labor (simple): 2,000 × $3.50 = $7,000
  • Total: $26,000 (~$13/sq ft)

Example 2: 3,000 sq ft Installation with Drainage Challenges

  • Premium turf: 3,000 × $10 = $30,000
  • Underlayment: 3,000 × $2.50 = $7,500
  • Drainage correction & soil prep: 3,000 × $2 = $6,000
  • Installation labor (complex): 3,000 × $5 = $15,000
  • Total: $58,500 (~$19.50/sq ft)

The Hidden Costs Most People Forget

Site Preparation

If your yard has existing grass that needs removal, old landscape features being relocated, or significant grading issues, these add cost. Typically $500-2,000 depending on the scope.

Infill Material

Most artificial turf uses infill (silica sand or rubber crumb) to weight it down and support the fibers. Initial infill is usually included in installation, but refill material might be needed every 3-5 years. Cost is modest ($100-300 per 1,000 sq ft) but worth budgeting.

Edging and Transitions

Professional aluminum or plastic edging around flower beds, patios, and driveways adds polish but costs $5-15 per linear foot.

The Real Conversation: Long-Term ROI

Here's what matters. Let's compare 15 years of ownership between artificial turf and maintaining natural grass in Georgia.

Artificial Turf (3,000 sq ft):
  • Installation: $36,000
  • Annual maintenance: ~$200/year × 15 = $3,000
  • Occasional infill: ~$400/year × 15 = $6,000
  • 15-Year Total: $45,000
Natural Grass (3,000 sq ft):
  • Initial sod: $4,500
  • Water bills: ~$1,200/year × 15 = $18,000
  • Fertilizer & weed control: ~$900/year × 15 = $13,500
  • Mowing/yard equipment: ~$600/year × 15 = $9,000
  • Disease treatments & repairs: ~$500/year × 15 = $7,500
  • Complete renovation (year 8): $6,000
  • 15-Year Total: $58,500

In this example, artificial turf saves you $13,500 over 15 years. And that's not even accounting for:

  • The value of your time (no mowing, fertilizing, treating disease)
  • Georgia's rising water costs (these could climb higher)
  • The peace of mind knowing your lawn looks perfect year-round
  • Potential property value increase from improved curb appeal

When is Artificial Turf NOT Worth It?

I believe in honest recommendations. Artificial turf might not be the right choice if:

  • You're in a rental property you'll only keep 2-3 years (not enough time for ROI)
  • You genuinely enjoy yard work and landscaping (some people do!)
  • Your budget is extremely limited and you have zero upfront capital
  • Your property has severe grading/drainage issues that would make installation extremely expensive

Otherwise? For most North Georgia homeowners, especially those tired of fighting our climate, it's the smarter financial move.
