DIY vs Professional Turf Installation: An Honest Comparison

By Dusty Broadhead | March 16, 2026

I get asked this question frequently: "Can I install artificial turf myself to save money?" The answer is honest: yes, technically you can attempt a DIY installation. But should you? Let me share what I've learned from 20+ years in this business, including the reality of DIY projects I've had to repair or replace.

The DIY Appeal

DIY artificial turf installation seems straightforward on the surface: remove old grass, prepare the ground, lay down turf, glue seams together. How hard could it be? The appeal is obvious—labor typically represents 40-50% of professional installation costs. Saving thousands of dollars is tempting.

What DIY Projects Usually Get Right

If someone does attempt DIY installation, they can often succeed with basic tasks: removing old grass and debris, laying turf in straightforward rectangular areas, basic seaming. For a small, simple project on relatively level property, DIY can work.

Where DIY Projects Usually Fail

Improper Base Preparation

This is the #1 cause of DIY failure. Artificial turf longevity depends entirely on proper base preparation. This requires understanding soil compaction, proper grading, drainage considerations, and Georgia's specific clay soils. Most DIYers skip this or do it inadequately. Result: turf that settles unevenly, develops low spots that collect water, fails prematurely.

Inadequate Drainage Solutions

Georgia rainfall is substantial. Proper drainage requires perimeter drains, gravel zones, and proper grading. DIY projects typically ignore this. The turf becomes a water-holding surface rather than draining properly. Problems develop quickly.

Seaming and Sealing Issues

Professional installers use specialized equipment and proven techniques for seaming turf. DIY seams often separate, allowing water infiltration and creating visible failures. Improper seaming is a common reason DIY projects fail.

Infill Management

DIY projects often don't understand infill requirements. They may use incorrect materials, apply improper amounts, or fail to establish proper infill distribution. Result: turf that doesn't perform or feel right, with premature settling and migration issues.

Edging and Containment

Professional installations include proper edging that prevents fiber migration and maintains borders. DIY projects often skip this. Edges fray, turf creeps outside its boundary, and the installation looks progressively worse.

Warranty and Support

DIY installations typically void manufacturer warranty. If the turf fails (which is likely given the above issues), you're responsible for replacement costs. Professional installations come with warranty coverage and professional support if issues arise.

Hidden Costs of DIY Projects

Equipment Rental

Proper base preparation requires compacting equipment, grading tools, and drainage installation tools. Renting these for a week might cost $1,000-$2,000—cutting significantly into "savings."

Turf Damage and Waste

Professional installers minimize waste. DIY projects typically waste 15-25% of materials due to cutting errors, installation mistakes, and improper handling. That's significant additional material costs.

Time and Physical Labor

A 1,000-square-foot professional installation takes 2-3 days with a crew. DIY might take 20-40 hours of personal labor—weekends consumed. Your labor is worth something even if you're not billing for it.

Correcting Mistakes

Failed seams, poor drainage, improper settling—these require expensive fixes. I've had clients spend $3,000-$5,000 fixing DIY installations that only cost $2,000 less initially. The "savings" disappear fast.

Real DIY Failure Examples

The Settling Problem

A Kennesaw homeowner installed artificial turf in their backyard. Six months later, portions had settled unevenly, creating low spots that pooled water after rain. Poor base preparation caused this. Fixing it required removing and reinstalling the affected areas—costing $1,500 more than professional installation would have been originally.

The Separation Disaster

Another project featured turf that separated along seams after the first heavy rain. Water infiltrated underneath. The turf had to be completely removed and reinstalled professionally. The "savings" were completely lost, plus they paid for a professional fix.

The Warranty Void

Yet another DIY installation had a manufacturing defect that appeared a year later. Because it was DIY-installed, the manufacturer wouldn't honor warranty. The homeowner replaced it at personal cost. Professional installation would have been covered.

When DIY Might Make Sense

Small, simple projects in ideal conditions might work: a small dog run area on existing level, well-draining property with straightforward rectangular dimensions and no complex seaming needs. Even then, understand the risks.

For any project over 400-500 square feet, or any property with grading, drainage, or complex layout considerations, professional installation is genuinely justified.

Professional Installation Advantages

Professional installers bring expertise, proper equipment, quality materials, and accountability. A typical professional Georgia installation costs $15-$25 per square foot. For 1,000 square feet, that's $15,000-$25,000. Sounds high until you compare it to DIY failure costs.

Professional installations come with warranties, proper base preparation, correct drainage solutions, expert seaming, and ongoing support. These aren't luxuries—they're protections that matter.

My Honest Recommendation

If you're handy and your project is genuinely small and simple, DIY might save money. But be realistic about the risks and difficulty. For most properties and projects, professional installation delivers results that justify the cost. You get a properly installed system that lasts, performs correctly, and comes with warranty support.
