Does Artificial Turf Increase Home Value? ROI Analysis

By Dusty Broadhead | February 12, 2026

One of the first questions homeowners ask when considering artificial turf is whether it’s a good investment. Will it add value to my home? Will buyers appreciate it if I sell? Let me give you the honest answer based on what I’ve seen in the North Georgia real estate market over the past two decades.

The Short Answer

Yes, artificial turf generally increases home value—but the ROI isn’t as straightforward as, say, a kitchen remodel. The value comes from three places: direct property value increase, annual cost savings, and faster sale time. Let me break down each one.

Direct Property Value Increase

Curb appeal matters in real estate, and a perfectly green, manicured-looking lawn has measurable impact on property value. Studies from the National Association of Realtors consistently show that quality landscaping adds 5-15% to property value. Artificial turf contributes to this by ensuring your lawn looks perfect year-round—no brown patches, no weeds, no seasonal decline.

In the North Georgia market specifically, we’ve seen homes with professional turf installations appraise higher than comparable homes with standard natural lawns. The exact premium varies by neighborhood and overall property quality, but real estate agents in the Kennesaw, Marietta, and Atlanta metro area consistently tell us that turf is viewed positively by buyers.

A reasonable estimate: a quality turf installation recovers 50-80% of its cost in direct property value increase. On a $30,000 installation, that’s $15,000-$24,000 in added value.

Annual Cost Savings

This is where the ROI math gets compelling. A Georgia homeowner with a typical lawn spends $2,000-$4,000 annually on water, fertilizer, pest control, mowing (whether DIY equipment costs or hired service), and occasional re-sodding. Artificial turf reduces that to $200-$500 per year for basic maintenance.

The annual savings of $1,500-$3,500 accumulate quickly. Over 10 years, you’ve saved $15,000-$35,000 in maintenance costs. Combined with the direct property value increase, the total ROI often exceeds 100% of your initial investment within 7-10 years.

Faster Sale Time

This benefit is harder to quantify but real. Homes with excellent curb appeal sell faster. In a competitive market like metro Atlanta, a home with a stunning, low-maintenance turf lawn stands out in listing photos and during showings. It communicates that the property is well-maintained and move-in ready.

Several of our past customers have reported that their turf was specifically mentioned by buyers as a selling point—particularly pet owners who were excited about not dealing with muddy grass, and busy professionals who appreciated the zero-maintenance appeal.

When Turf Doesn’t Add Value

I believe in being honest about the limitations. Artificial turf may not add value (or could even detract) in certain situations:

  • Rural properties with acreage: If you’re selling a 5-acre property in Cherokee County, buyers expect natural landscape. Artificial turf on a large rural lot can look out of place.
  • Ultra-high-end neighborhoods: In some exclusive communities, buyers may prefer natural, professionally maintained gardens and lawns. Know your market.
  • Poor-quality installations: Cheap turf that looks obviously fake, has visible seams, or has faded from UV damage can actually hurt property value. Quality matters enormously.
  • HOA restrictions: Some Georgia HOAs still have rules about artificial turf. Always check before installing, or you could face removal requirements that destroy your investment.

The Pet Owner Premium

Here’s an interesting market trend: as more families have dogs (pet ownership in Georgia is above the national average), homes with pet-friendly features command a premium. A dedicated pet turf area with proper drainage is increasingly seen as a desirable amenity, similar to a fenced yard or mudroom. Dog owners will pay more for a home that already solves the “muddy paw” problem.

Putting Green ROI

Backyard putting greens are a special case. They’re a luxury feature that appeals to a specific buyer pool—golfers. If you’re in a neighborhood where golf culture is strong (and in North Georgia, there are many), a quality putting green can add significant appeal and value. But in a neighborhood where nobody golfs, it’s a nice-to-have that may not move the needle on price.

Our recommendation: install a putting green because you’ll enjoy it, and view any resale value as a bonus rather than the primary justification.

Maximizing Your Turf ROI

To get the best return on your turf investment:

  • Invest in quality installation. The difference between a $8/sq ft installation and a $12/sq ft installation is dramatic in appearance and longevity. The cheaper option costs you more in the long run.
  • Choose realistic-looking products. Multi-colored turf with thatch layers looks natural. Single-color, bright-green turf screams “fake” and turns off buyers.
  • Maintain it. Even though turf is low-maintenance, basic care (brushing, rinsing, infill top-ups) keeps it looking sharp for years.
  • Integrate with landscaping. Turf surrounded by beautiful hardscape, plants, and borders looks intentional and high-end. Turf plopped in an otherwise neglected yard doesn’t carry the same impact.
  • Keep documentation. Save your installation details, warranty info, and maintenance records. Buyers appreciate knowing the quality and age of the installation.

Artificial turf is one of the better landscaping investments you can make for a Georgia home. It’s not going to double your home’s value overnight, but the combination of property value increase, annual cost savings, and lifestyle improvement makes it a smart financial decision for most homeowners in our market.
