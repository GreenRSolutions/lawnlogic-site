Artificial Turf for Playgrounds: Safety and Benefits

By Dusty Broadhead | February 20, 2026

If you’re a parent in Georgia, you know the playground struggle. Mulch playgrounds get soggy and grow mold after rain. Rubber surfaces get scorching hot in summer. Sand gets everywhere—in shoes, clothes, eyes. And natural grass under playground equipment? It gets destroyed within weeks, leaving bare dirt that turns to mud every time it rains.

Artificial turf solves all of these problems, and it’s becoming the standard surface for both residential backyard play areas and commercial playgrounds across North Georgia. Here’s what you need to know.

Safety First: Fall Protection

The most important factor for any playground surface is fall attenuation—how well it absorbs impact when a child falls. Artificial turf, when installed with proper shock-absorbing padding underneath, meets and exceeds ASTM F1292 standards for playground safety.

The key is the pad layer. Standard turf installation for lawns uses a compacted aggregate base, which is hard. For playgrounds, we install a foam shock pad (typically 1-2 inches of closed-cell foam) between the base and the turf. This pad absorbs impact energy, significantly reducing the risk of head injuries from falls.

The critical height rating—the maximum height from which a child can fall without risk of life-threatening head injury—depends on the pad thickness. With a 1-inch pad, you typically get a critical fall height of 6-8 feet. With a 2-inch pad, that extends to 10-12 feet. Most residential play structures fall within the 6-8 foot range, so a standard 1-inch pad is usually sufficient.

Benefits Over Traditional Playground Surfaces

vs. Engineered Wood Fiber (Mulch)

Mulch is the most common playground surface because it’s cheap. But it decomposes, gets kicked out of fall zones, harbors insects and mold, and becomes a soggy mess in Georgia’s rainy seasons. It needs to be replenished regularly and can conceal sharp objects. Turf stays consistent, doesn’t decompose, drains instantly, and never needs replenishment.

vs. Poured Rubber

Poured rubber surfaces are excellent for safety but cost significantly more than turf and get extremely hot in direct sun—surface temperatures can reach 150°F+ on a Georgia summer day. Turf also gets warm in sun, but not nearly as hot as dark rubber, especially if you choose a product with cooling technology.

vs. Sand

Sand provides decent fall protection but creates an accessibility nightmare. Wheelchairs can’t traverse it, it gets in everything, cats use it as a litter box, and it compacts over time, reducing its cushioning ability. Turf with padding provides better fall protection while remaining fully accessible.

vs. Natural Grass

Natural grass under play equipment dies. Period. The shade from the structure and the concentrated foot traffic kill it within weeks. Then you have bare dirt, which becomes mud, which becomes a mess. Turf handles heavy foot traffic and shade without any degradation.

ADA Accessibility

This is a big deal for commercial playgrounds, churches, schools, and public facilities. Artificial turf with proper installation meets ADA accessibility requirements for playground surfaces. Wheelchair users can navigate across turf surfaces, which isn’t possible with mulch, sand, or loose gravel. For any publicly accessible playground, ADA compliance isn’t optional—it’s the law.

Year-Round Usability in Georgia

Georgia’s weather creates distinct challenges for each season. In summer, you want a surface that doesn’t become dangerously hot. In spring, you need something that handles pollen and rain. In fall, leaves need to be easily removed. In our mild winters, you want a surface that doesn’t become frozen solid or slippery.

Turf handles all of these. It drains almost instantly after rain, so kids can play 15 minutes after a downpour. It doesn’t collect or hold pollen—a quick rinse clears it. Leaves blow off easily. And in cold weather, turf remains soft and usable even when natural grass goes dormant and turns to slippery mud.

Installation for Playground Areas

Playground turf installation differs from standard lawn installation in several ways:

  • Shock pad required: A foam pad layer must be installed under the turf in the fall zone around all equipment. The pad specification depends on the equipment height.
  • Extended fall zones: CPSC guidelines require fall zones extending 6 feet in all directions from play equipment. Some equipment (like swings) requires even larger zones. The turf and pad must cover these entire areas.
  • Enhanced drainage: Playground areas see concentrated foot traffic that can displace infill. We use heavier infill application and more frequent securing along high-traffic paths.
  • Seamless design: We minimize seams in playground installations because children are more likely to catch feet on seam edges. Where seams are necessary, they’re reinforced and placed away from high-activity zones.

Cost for Playground Turf

Playground turf installations cost more than standard lawn turf because of the required shock pad and the additional engineering involved. Budget roughly $12-$20 per square foot for a complete playground turf installation in North Georgia, compared to $8-$15 for standard lawn turf. The cost varies based on pad thickness, turf product selection, and site complexity.

For a typical residential backyard play area (300-500 sq ft), you’re looking at $4,000-$10,000. For larger commercial playgrounds, costs scale accordingly but often include volume discounts on materials.

Maintenance for Playground Turf

Playground turf maintenance is similar to regular turf care: weekly debris removal, monthly rinsing, and periodic brushing. One additional consideration is checking the infill level in high-traffic areas—kids running, jumping, and sliding can displace infill faster than normal foot traffic. Top off infill quarterly in the most active zones.

Also inspect the turf surface periodically for any damage, especially around equipment mounting points. Kids are creative about finding ways to stress surfaces, and catching small issues early prevents them from becoming expensive repairs.

For any parent or organization considering a playground surface in Georgia, artificial turf with proper shock padding is the best combination of safety, usability, and long-term value. It’s an investment that pays off every time your kids play outside—rain or shine, 365 days a year.
